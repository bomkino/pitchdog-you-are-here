# Code of conduct

This project is for people making things under uncertainty. The community should reduce unnecessary fear, not reproduce the ugliest parts of the industries around us.

## Expected

- Critique work specifically; treat people as more than their output.
- Make room for people historically pushed out of film, technology, design, and capital.
- Respect names, pronouns, boundaries, access needs, and different levels of formal experience.
- Credit labor and references. Do not quietly absorb somebody else’s work into your authority.
- Challenge harmful assumptions and power imbalances without turning disagreement into spectacle.
- Accept correction, repair what you can, and move on without demanding emotional caretaking from the person affected.

## Not welcome

Harassment, threats, stalking, doxxing, sexualized attention, intimidation, racism, casteism, sexism, misogyny, homophobia, transphobia, ableism, xenophobia, class contempt, religious bigotry, professional retaliation, coercive networking, exploiting unpaid labor, publishing private material without consent, spam, and bad-faith disruption are not welcome here.

## Reporting

For a security issue, use [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). For conduct concerns, contact the repository owner privately through [pitch.dog](https://pitch.dog/) rather than opening a public issue. Include only what is needed to understand the incident.

Maintainers may edit, remove, lock, or reject contributions and participation that violate this policy. Enforcement should protect affected people, preserve privacy, and avoid punitive theater.
